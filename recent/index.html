Map of New Cases

Click and zoom in on the map for more details.

The map shows the rate of new COVID-19 cases confirmed over a 30 day period covering through the most recent reported data. Rates are calculated as the number of new cases per 10,000 residents. The map initially displays the rates by neighborhood, zoom in to see rates for smaller areas (census tracts).​

Areas with fewer than twenty cases

Because of variability in the data, the rate of new cases is not calculated for areas with fewer than twenty new cases. These areas are shaded with a grey dotted pattern.​

Privacy protections

To protect the privacy of residents, the City does not disclose the number of new confirmed cases in areas where the total cumulative cases is less than 10. In addition, no case data is shown for areas with resident populations of fewer than 1,000 people. These areas are dark grey on the map.​

Map updates reflect underlying data

This map displays new case rates to show differences in rates across the City. The map legend and colors are based on the underlying distribution of the data, which is updated daily. The map legend for both neighborhood and census tracts may adjust as the data distributions change.​
